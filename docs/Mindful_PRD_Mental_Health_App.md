# **1. Executive Summary**
Depression affects over 280 million people worldwide, yet many struggle to access adequate support and resources. Mindful is a compassionate mobile application designed to provide comprehensive mental health support for individuals experiencing depression. The app serves as a trusted companion throughout the user's mental wellness journey, offering evidence-based tools for mood tracking, coping strategies, peer support, and professional resources.

This Product Requirements Document outlines the design and development requirements for a hackathon prototype that demonstrates the core value proposition and user experience of Mindful. The solution prioritizes user safety, privacy, accessibility, and emotional well-being while providing actionable tools for managing depression symptoms and fostering hope.
## **Key Highlights**
- Calming, empathetic UI/UX designed specifically for users experiencing emotional distress
- Evidence-based features including mood tracking, CBT-informed coping strategies, and goal setting
- Secure peer support community with moderation and safety features
- Direct access to professional mental health resources and crisis support
- Full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance for accessibility
- Privacy-first architecture with end-to-end encryption for sensitive data

# **3. Problem Statement**
## **The Challenge**
Depression is one of the most prevalent mental health conditions globally, affecting over 280 million people. Despite its widespread impact, individuals facing depression encounter numerous barriers to receiving adequate support.
## **Current Barriers**
- **Limited Access to Professional Care:** Long wait times, high costs, and geographic barriers prevent many from accessing therapists or psychiatrists
- **Lack of Daily Support Tools:** Between therapy sessions, individuals often lack practical tools for managing symptoms
- **Social Isolation:** Stigma and depression symptoms can lead to withdrawal, reducing access to peer support
- **Information Overload:** Overwhelming and conflicting mental health information makes it hard to find reliable resources
- **Difficulty Tracking Progress:** Without systematic tracking, recognizing patterns and improvements becomes challenging
- **Crisis Moments:** During acute episodes, finding immediate support can be difficult and time-sensitive
## **User Needs**
Individuals experiencing depression need:

- A safe, private space to express and track their emotions
- Evidence-based coping strategies that are accessible anytime
- Connection with others who understand their experiences
- Clear pathways to professional help when needed
- Tools to set and achieve small, manageable goals
- Reliable mental health education and resources
## **Solution Overview**
Mindful addresses these challenges by providing a comprehensive, accessible mobile application that combines self-help tools, peer support, and professional resources. The app acts as a compassionate companion throughout the user's mental wellness journey, offering immediate support while facilitating connections to long-term professional care.

# **5. Product Vision & Goals**
## **Vision Statement**
*To create a compassionate digital companion that empowers individuals experiencing depression to take control of their mental wellness journey through evidence-based tools, supportive community, and seamless access to professional care.*
## **Product Goals**
### **Primary Goals**
1. **Provide Daily Support:** Offer accessible, evidence-based tools for managing depression symptoms on a daily basis
1. **Foster Self-Awareness:** Enable users to track mood patterns and identify triggers through intuitive tracking features
1. **Build Community:** Create safe spaces for peer support and reduce isolation through moderated community features
1. **Bridge to Professional Care:** Facilitate connections to mental health professionals and crisis resources

### **Success Criteria**
- Users report feeling more in control of their mental health
- Increased user awareness of mood patterns and triggers

## **Core Principles**
- **Safety First:** User safety and crisis prevention are paramount
- **Privacy Protected:** All personal data is encrypted and never shared without explicit consent
- **Evidence-Based:** Features grounded in clinical research and best practices
- **Non-Judgmental:** Language and design that validates experiences without stigma
- **Accessible to All:** Inclusive design that works for users with varying abilities


# **6. Core Features & Requirements**
This section details the eight core features of Mindful, including functional requirements, user interactions, and technical specifications.
## **6.1 Mood Tracking**
### **Feature Description**
A daily mood tracking system that allows users to record their emotional states with context and visualize patterns over time. This feature helps users develop self-awareness and provides valuable data for discussions with mental health professionals.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Daily mood entry with customizable emotion scale (1-10 or emotion faces)
- Multiple check-ins per day capability
- Optional contextual tags (e.g., sleep quality, medication taken, exercise, social interaction)
- Note-taking field for additional context
- Visual representations: line graphs, calendar heat maps, mood distribution charts
- Pattern detection: identifying correlations between activities and mood
- Export capability to PDF for sharing with healthcare providers
- Gentle reminders (customizable frequency) to log mood
### **User Interactions**
1. User opens app and is greeted with "How are you feeling today?"
1. User selects mood level using intuitive slider or emotion icons
1. User optionally adds contextual tags and notes
1. System saves entry with timestamp
1. User can view historical data through various visualizations
### **Design Considerations**
- Quick entry (< 30 seconds) to reduce friction
- Color-coded visualizations using calming palette
- Avoid judgmental language (no "good" or "bad" days)
- Option to set private entries not visible in shared reports

## **6.2 Coping Strategies Library**
### **Feature Description**
A curated library of evidence-based coping strategies and self-help techniques drawn from CBT, DBT, mindfulness, and other therapeutic approaches. Users can access these resources anytime to manage symptoms.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Categorized content: Mindfulness, Cognitive Techniques, Physical Activities, Relaxation, Emergency Grounding
- Search and filter functionality
- Favorites/bookmarking system
- Guided audio exercises (breathing, meditation, progressive muscle relaxation)
- Text-based techniques with step-by-step instructions
- Video demonstrations for complex techniques
- Suggested strategies based on current mood (if mood tracking enabled)
- User rating system to personalize recommendations
### **Content Categories**

|**Category**|**Example Techniques**|
| :- | :- |
|Mindfulness|5-4-3-2-1 grounding, body scan, mindful breathing|
|Cognitive|Thought challenging, cognitive reframing, gratitude practice|
|Physical|Gentle yoga, walking meditation, stretching routines|
|Relaxation|Progressive muscle relaxation, guided imagery, calming sounds|
|Crisis|Safety planning, distraction techniques, emergency contacts|

## **6.3 Goal Setting & Progress Tracking**
### **Feature Description**
A goal-setting framework that helps users establish and track achievable daily, weekly, and long-term goals that contribute to mental wellness and recovery. Emphasizes small, manageable steps.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Goal creation with SMART framework guidance (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Pre-defined goal templates for common wellness activities
- Custom goal creation
- Daily check-in for goal progress
- Visual progress tracking (completion bars, streaks, milestone celebrations)
- Gentle reminders (not pushy or guilt-inducing)
- Celebration of achievements with positive reinforcement
- Reflection prompts for missed goals (learning opportunity, not punishment)
### **Example Goal Templates**
- Get outside for 10 minutes today
- Practice deep breathing for 5 minutes
- Reach out to one friend or family member
- Complete one small task on my to-do list
- Get 7+ hours of sleep tonight

## **6.4 Peer Support Community**
### **Feature Description**
A moderated community forum where users can connect with others experiencing depression, share experiences, offer support, and reduce feelings of isolation. Safety and moderation are paramount.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Topic-based discussion forums (e.g., "Living with Depression," "Coping Strategies," "Recovery Stories")
- Anonymous posting option with pseudonyms
- Moderation system: AI pre-screening + human moderators
- Community guidelines prominently displayed
- Reporting mechanism for concerning content
- Upvoting/helpful marking system for supportive comments
- Direct messaging disabled (safety measure)
- Trigger warning tags for sensitive content
- Search functionality for finding relevant discussions
### **Safety Features**
- AI detection of crisis language triggers immediate moderator alert
- Automatic display of crisis resources when crisis language detected
- Blocking and reporting functionality
- Zero tolerance for harmful advice or triggering content
- Regular mental health professional oversight of community dynamics

## **6.5 Professional Support Access**
### **Feature Description**
Direct pathways to professional mental health support including therapist directories, teletherapy options, and 24/7 crisis hotlines. Bridges the gap between self-help and professional care.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Therapist directory with filters (specialty, location, insurance, language, modality)
- Integration with teletherapy platforms (future: in-app booking)
- Crisis hotline access: one-tap calling to National Suicide Prevention Lifeline, Crisis Text Line
- Local emergency services contact (911/local equivalent)
- Crisis chat functionality (24/7 text-based support)
- Information about different types of mental health professionals
- Insurance and payment assistance information
- Preparation guide for first therapy appointment
### **Crisis Support Accessibility**
- Crisis resources accessible from every screen (persistent help button)
- No login required to access crisis features
- Multiple contact modalities (call, text, chat)

## **6.6 Medication Reminders**
### **Feature Description**
A medication tracking and reminder system for users on prescribed medications, helping maintain consistency in medication adherence.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Multiple medication entries
- Customizable reminder times (multiple times per day)
- Snooze functionality
- Tracking of taken/missed doses
- Refill reminders based on supply tracking
- Adherence statistics and visualizations
- Notes field for side effects or observations
- Export adherence report for doctor appointments
### **Privacy & Security**
- Medication data encrypted at rest and in transit
- Optional: Hide medication names from lock screen notifications
- Data never shared with third parties

## **6.7 Private Journaling**
### **Feature Description**
A secure, encrypted journaling space for users to document thoughts, feelings, and experiences. Promotes self-reflection and emotional processing.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Free-form text entry
- Prompted journaling option (therapeutic prompts)
- Voice-to-text capability
- Tagging system for categorizing entries
- Search functionality across entries
- Mood association with journal entries
- Gratitude journal section
- Export capability (PDF, text)
### **Therapeutic Prompts Examples**
- What am I grateful for today?
- Describe a moment when I felt calm
- What triggered my mood change today?
- A small victory I had today was...
- How did I show myself kindness today?
### **Security**
- End-to-end encryption
- Optional additional PIN/biometric lock for journal section
- No cloud backup by default (user opt-in with clear warnings)

## **6.8 Educational Resources Hub**
### **Feature Description**
A comprehensive library of evidence-based educational content about depression, mental health, and wellness. Empowers users with knowledge and reduces stigma.
### **Functional Requirements**
- Categorized content library (Understanding Depression, Treatment Options, Self-Care, Recovery Stories)
- Multiple content formats: articles, videos, infographics, audio
- Scientifically vetted information with source citations
- Reading progress tracking
- Bookmarking functionality
- Share articles (external sharing)
- Search and filter by topic, format, reading time
- Recommended reading based on user profile and activity
### **Content Categories**
- **Understanding Depression:** Symptoms, causes, types, neuroscience basics
- **Treatment Options:** Therapy types, medications, alternative treatments
- **Self-Care & Wellness:** Sleep, nutrition, exercise, social connection
- **Recovery Stories:** First-person narratives, hope-building content
- **Supporting Loved Ones:** How to help someone with depression

# **8. Design Principles**
These principles guide every design decision in Mindful, ensuring the app truly serves users experiencing depression with empathy and effectiveness.
## **1. Calming & Soothing Interface**
- Soft color palette (blues, teals, lavenders) avoiding harsh contrasts
- Generous white space to reduce visual overwhelm
- Smooth animations and transitions (never jarring or sudden)
- Rounded corners and organic shapes over sharp angles
- Consistent, predictable layouts
## **2. Low Cognitive Load**
- Clear hierarchy with one primary action per screen
- Minimal text (concise, scannable)
- Progressive disclosure (show information as needed)
- Simple navigation with maximum 2 taps to any feature
- Default states and smart suggestions to reduce decision fatigue
## **3. Empathetic Language**
- Never judgmental or prescriptive
- Validating and understanding tone
- Avoiding clinical jargon where possible
- No guilt-inducing messages for missed activities
- Person-first language ("person with depression" not "depressed person")
- Hopeful framing without toxic positivity
## **4. Privacy & Safety by Design**
- End-to-end encryption for sensitive data
- Clear privacy controls easily accessible
- No data selling or third-party sharing
- Crisis resources always one tap away
- Automatic detection of crisis language with appropriate responses
## **5. Encouraging Progress Without Pressure**
- Celebrate small wins meaningfully
- Reminders are gentle suggestions, never demands
- No streaks or punitive gamification
- Acknowledge that progress is non-linear
- Allow users to set their own pace
## **6. Accessibility First**
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Screen reader optimization
- Adjustable text sizes and contrast
- Keyboard navigation support
- Reduced motion options
- Multiple input modalities (tap, voice, type)


# **9. Accessibility Requirements**
Mindful must be fully accessible to users with disabilities, including those with visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive impairments. Accessibility is not an afterthought but a core requirement.
## **Visual Accessibility**

|**Requirement**|**Implementation**|
| :- | :- |
|Color Contrast|Minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text (WCAG AA)|
|Text Sizing|Support up to 200% text scaling without horizontal scrolling|
|Screen Readers|All UI elements labeled with descriptive alt text and ARIA labels|
|Color Independence|Information never conveyed by color alone (use icons, labels, patterns)|

## **Auditory Accessibility**
- All audio content (meditation guides, etc.) has text transcripts
- Video content includes closed captions
- Visual alternatives for all audio notifications
## **Motor Accessibility**
- All touch targets minimum 44x44 pixels
- Adequate spacing between interactive elements
- No time-limited interactions (or generous time limits with extensions)
- Voice input support where applicable
- Full keyboard navigation support
## **Cognitive Accessibility**
- Plain language (8th-grade reading level or lower)
- Consistent navigation and layout patterns
- Clear error messages with recovery guidance
- Option to disable animations for users sensitive to motion
- Focus mode option that simplifies interface
## **Additional Features**
- **Text-to-Speech:** Option to have content read aloud
- **Dyslexia-Friendly Font:** Optional OpenDyslexic or similar font
- **Dark Mode:** Reduces eye strain, respects system settings
- **Customizable Interface:** Allow users to adjust colors, text size, spacing

# **11. Success Metrics**
Measuring the success of Mindful goes beyond typical app metrics. While engagement is important, the true measure is impact on user wellbeing.
## **User Engagement Metrics**
- **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Target: 60% of registered users
- **Session Length:** Average 5-10 minutes per session
- **Feature Adoption:** % of users utilizing each core feature
- **Retention Rate:** 30-day retention > 40%, 90-day > 25%
## **Clinical Impact Metrics**
- **Mood Trend Improvement:** % of users showing upward mood trends over 4 weeks
- **Professional Help Seeking:** % of users who connect with therapists through app
- **Medication Adherence:** % increase in reported medication consistency
- **Goal Achievement Rate:** % of set goals completed
## **User Satisfaction Metrics**
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Target > 40
- **User Satisfaction Score:** Target > 4.5/5
- **User Testimonials:** Qualitative feedback on impact
- **Feature Usefulness Ratings:** Individual feature satisfaction
## **Safety Metrics**
- Crisis resource utilization (how many users accessed crisis support)
- Moderation effectiveness (harmful content removal time)
- User safety incident reports
## **Hackathon-Specific Success Criteria**
- Completed functional prototype demonstrating 4-5 core features
- Clear, compelling visual design aligned with brand identity
- Evidence of user-centered design thinking
- Accessibility features implemented
- Strong presentation demonstrating real-world impact potential

# **14. Risks & Mitigation**

|**Risk**|**Mitigation**|
| :- | :- |
|User experiencing crisis without immediate professional help|<p>• Prominent crisis button on all screens</p><p>• AI detection of concerning language</p><p>• Immediate connection to hotlines</p><p>• Clear disclaimer that app is not emergency service</p>|
|Harmful advice shared in community|<p>• Active moderation (AI + human)</p><p>• Clear community guidelines</p><p>• User reporting system</p><p>• Mental health professional oversight</p>|
|User substituting app for professional treatment|<p>• Clear messaging that app is supplemental</p><p>• Regular prompts to seek professional help</p><p>• Easy access to therapist directory</p>|

## **Privacy & Security Risks**

|**Risk**|**Mitigation**|
| :- | :- |
|Data breach exposing sensitive mental health information|<p>• End-to-end encryption</p><p>• Regular security audits</p><p>• Penetration testing</p><p>• HIPAA compliance if applicable</p>|
|User privacy concerns reducing engagement|<p>• Transparent privacy policy</p><p>• User control over data sharing</p><p>• Anonymous community posting option</p><p>• No selling of user data</p>|
